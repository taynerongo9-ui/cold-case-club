The Hargrove Disappearance

Ashford, Oregon Winter 1987 Missing Person

Eleanor Hargrove, 34, was last seen leaving the Monarch Theatre on a January night. Her coat was found draped over the railing of Morrison Creek Bridge. Her car sat alone in the theatre parking lot. No body was ever recovered.

Police called it a probable suicide. The case was closed in six weeks. But Eleanor's mother never believed it. Neither did the bartender who served her that night. Neither did the detective who retired with this file on his desk.

Three witnesses. Three different stories. One of them is lying.

The Lighthouse Keeper's Wife

Crestfall, Maine March 1962 Suspicious Death

Catherine Marsh was found at the base of the cliffs below Dunmore Point Lighthouse on a cold March morning. The constable wrote "accidental fall in dense fog" and closed the case the same week.

But the lighthouse log tells a different story. The fog had cleared hours before Catherine fell. The night was clear. The path was familiar. She'd walked it a thousand times.

A hidden diary. A sister-in-law with a borrowed name. A shipping fortune hanging in the balance. The sea keeps its secrets — but the lighthouse keeper's wife left hers in the walls.
