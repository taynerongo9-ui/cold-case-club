# COLD CASE CLUB — Marketing Video Script
## "Warren Buffett Explains Why This Business Prints Money"
### YouTube Long-Form (~12-15 min) — Promotional / Launch Video

---

### HOOK (0:00 - 0:30)

**[WARREN — sitting at a desk. Single lamp. Manila folder in front of him. Cherry Coke.]**

WARREN:
In 1987, a woman named Eleanor Hargrove walked out of the Monarch Theatre and was never seen again.

Three witnesses. Three different stories. A husband who lawyered up before the search party came home.

**[He slides the folder toward the camera]**

WARREN:
This case has been cold for thirty-nine years. And for seventy-five dollars, I'm going to let you try to solve it.

**[Beat. He takes a sip of Cherry Coke.]**

WARREN:
My name is Warren Buffett. And I'm here to tell you about the best seventy-five dollar investment you'll make this year.

**[TITLE CARD: COLD CASE CLUB — A Mystery In Your Mailbox]**

---

### THE PROBLEM (0:30 - 2:00)

**[Warren, conversational]**

WARREN:
Let me ask you something. When was the last time you were genuinely excited to check your mailbox?

Not your email. Your actual, physical mailbox. The one at the end of your driveway.

I'll bet it's been a while. Because what's in there? Bills. Catalogs you didn't ask for. Political mailers from people you've never heard of.

Your mailbox has become a chore.

But it didn't used to be. When I was young, getting a letter was an event. You'd see the envelope, you'd wonder who sent it, you'd tear it open. There was anticipation. Surprise. Delight.

That feeling didn't go away because people changed. It went away because nobody sends anything worth opening anymore.

Cold Case Club changes that.

---

### WHAT IT IS (2:00 - 5:00)

**[Warren holds up a thick envelope, stamped "CLASSIFIED"]**

WARREN:
Here's how it works. And I want to be clear — this is simple. I like simple.

You sign up. You pick a case. And every two weeks, an evidence packet arrives in your mailbox.

**[He opens the envelope, starts spreading items on the desk]**

WARREN:
Not a box of trinkets. Not a subscription to something you'll forget to cancel. Evidence.

Real, physical documents that you hold in your hands.

**[Holds up each item]**

WARREN:
Detective's case notes. The kind with coffee rings and margin scribbles and crossed-out theories.

Witness statements. Contradictory ones. Because witnesses lie, and part of your job is figuring out who.

Newspaper clippings from the era. Headlines. Obituaries. Society pages with names you'll learn to pay attention to.

Photographs. Maps. Diagrams. Things you can pin to a wall or spread across a dining room table.

And coded messages. Because sometimes the best clues aren't handed to you. You have to earn them.

**[Leans back]**

WARREN:
Over six months, twelve packets arrive. Twelve deliveries of new evidence, new leads, new suspects. And by the end, you have everything you need to answer one question:

Who did it?

The final packet — month six — reveals the truth. You submit your theory before you open it. And you find out if you were right.

Now — I've been analyzing businesses for seventy years. And I don't use the word "elegant" lightly. But this? This is an elegant product.

---

### WHY IT'S THE PERFECT GIFT (5:00 - 7:30)

**[Warren, warmer tone]**

WARREN:
Now, I want to talk about something that the smartest business people understand. And that's this: the best products aren't bought for yourself. They're bought for someone else.

Think about See's Candies. I've owned that company since 1972. You know when See's sells the most chocolate? Not when someone walks in and says, "I'd like some chocolate." It's when someone walks in and says, "I need a gift for my mother."

Cold Case Club works the same way.

You know someone who listens to true crime podcasts on every car ride. Who binges detective shows. Who reads mystery novels in bed. You've been looking for a gift for this person, and everything you think of is either boring or they already have it.

This is the gift.

Seventy-five dollars. Six months of mail they'll actually look forward to. Every two weeks, they'll go to the mailbox and think of you.

Not once. Twelve times.

**[Slight smile]**

WARREN:
And here's a detail I appreciate from a business standpoint: the person you gift it to can't easily cancel. They didn't sign up. There's no login. There's no "manage subscription" button. They just receive evidence in the mail. That's a structurally low churn rate. And I do love a low churn rate.

But more importantly — it means the gift just keeps giving. Mother's Day, birthdays, Christmas, anniversaries. Any time you'd normally spend fifty dollars on a gift card they'll forget about in a drawer, spend seventy-five on something they'll talk about for years.

---

### SOCIAL PROOF (7:30 - 9:00)

**[Warren reading from cards, as if reviewing testimonials]**

WARREN:
Don't take my word for it. Let me read you what investigators — that's what we call our subscribers — have said.

Sarah K. writes: "I bought this for my mom on a whim. She called me after the second packet. She's got a suspect board pinned to her office wall. I've never seen her this excited."

A suspect board. In her office. That is customer engagement.

Diana M.: "My book club switched from novels to this. We meet every two weeks to go through the evidence together."

So one subscription turned into a shared experience for an entire group. That's organic growth right there. One customer, eight people engaged.

James R.: "The coded message in month three took me a full weekend to crack. Already signed up for Case number two."

A full weekend on one clue. And he immediately bought again. That's a repeat customer with high lifetime value.

**[Sets cards down]**

WARREN:
You know, when I evaluate a business, I look for customer love. Not customer satisfaction — that's table stakes. Love. The kind where they tell their friends unprompted. The kind where they buy again without being asked.

That's what I see here.

---

### PRICING & CTA (9:00 - 11:30)

**[Warren, direct]**

WARREN:
Let me talk numbers. Because I always talk numbers.

There are two ways to join Cold Case Club.

Option one: monthly. Fourteen ninety-nine a month. You pay as you investigate. Six months, twelve packets, cancel anytime. Total cost over the full case: about ninety dollars.

Option two — and this is the one I'd choose — the full case, prepaid. Seventy-four ninety-nine. One payment. You save fifteen dollars. You get an exclusive storage tin to keep all your evidence in. And you've committed — which, in my experience, makes everything more enjoyable.

**[Warren taps the desk]**

WARREN:
Now, seventy-five dollars for six months of entertainment. Let me put that in perspective.

A dinner out for two? That's seventy-five dollars for ninety minutes.
Two movie tickets with popcorn? Forty dollars for two hours.
A streaming subscription you barely use? A hundred and eighty dollars a year.

This is seventy-five dollars for six months of engagement. Of anticipation. Of checking your mailbox with actual excitement. Of spreading evidence on your kitchen table and arguing with your spouse about whether the husband did it.

That's roughly twelve dollars and fifty cents per month. Less than your daily coffee.

**[He pauses]**

WARREN:
And if you're buying it as a gift — which most people do — it's the most memorable seventy-five dollars you'll spend all year. I guarantee the person you give this to will talk about it more than anything else you've ever given them.

---

### CLOSE (11:30 - 13:00)

**[Warren, reflective, slower]**

WARREN:
You know, I've spent my career looking for value. Underpriced assets. Businesses that deliver more than they cost. Experiences that compound.

A mystery in your mailbox is a small thing. But small things that bring people joy — real, tangible, hold-it-in-your-hands joy — those aren't small at all. Those are the things people remember.

In a world of infinite scrolling and five-second attention spans, someone is going to sit at their kitchen table for an hour, reading a witness statement, trying to figure out if the bartender was lying about what she saw.

That's not entertainment. That's immersion. And there's very little of it left.

**[He picks up the manila folder, holds it toward the camera]**

WARREN:
The Hargrove case has been cold for thirty-nine years.

Your first evidence packet ships within three days of signing up.

**[Sets it down. Direct to camera.]**

WARREN:
The case is at coldcaseclub.com. I'd start before the trail gets any colder.

**[He picks up the Cherry Coke. Takes a sip. Slight nod.]**

**[Fade to black.]**

**[End card: COLDCASECLUB.COM — with Subscribe, Gift a Case buttons]**

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Video Style
- Single camera, minimal cuts — let Warren talk
- Warm, low-key lighting (desk lamp, amber tones)
- Physical props on desk: manila folder, evidence packets, Cherry Coke
- No flashy graphics — use the physical evidence AS the visuals
- When he holds up items, camera pushes in slightly

### Props Needed
- Manila folder stamped "CLASSIFIED"
- 3-4 sample evidence packets (detective notes, newspaper clippings, witness statements, coded message)
- Evidence storage tin with Cold Case Club branding
- Cherry Coke (glass bottle if possible)
- Reading glasses (optional — Buffett detail)
- Simple wooden desk, leather chair, single lamp

### Audio
- No background music during monologue — just Warren's voice
- Subtle ambient room tone
- Optional: soft, tense score during the opening hook only (first 30 seconds)
- Sound design: envelope opening, paper rustling when he handles evidence

### Tone Targets
- Not campy, not cheesy — sophisticated and understated
- Buffett's persuasion style: folksy math ("$75 for six months vs $75 for a dinner")
- He's recommending this like he'd recommend a stock — with conviction backed by logic
- Warm but direct — he respects the viewer's intelligence

### Key Metrics to Display On Screen
- "$74.99 for 6 months" when discussing pricing
- "12 Evidence Packets" during the what-it-is section
- "coldcaseclub.com" persistent in lower third during final 90 seconds

### Suggested Thumbnail
- Warren at desk, holding manila folder toward camera
- Folder stamped "CLASSIFIED" in red
- Text overlay: "The $75 Investment Everyone's Talking About"
- Dark background, single lamp light on face

### Distribution Plan
1. YouTube (full 13-min version)
2. Cut :60 and :30 versions for Meta/Instagram ads
3. Cut the "Gift" section (5:00-7:30) as a standalone Mother's Day ad
4. Audio-only version for podcast ad reads
