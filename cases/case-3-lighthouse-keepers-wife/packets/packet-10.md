[PRINTED ON: Police report form, Maine State seal.]

**STATE OF MAINE**

**MOTOR VEHICLE ACCIDENT REPORT**

**Case No.:** WPD-58-0892
**Date of Accident:** October 14, 1958
**Time:** Approximately 3:40 PM
**Location:** Route 1, 2.3 miles south of Wiscasset village
**Reporting Officer:** Patrolman David C. Reynolds

---

**VEHICLE:**
- 1955 Ford Fairlane, two-door sedan, blue
- Massachusetts registration: 58-MK-4127
- Registered to: Helen R. Marsh, 31 Revere St., Boston, MA
- Condition after accident: Total loss. Front end compressed approximately 3 feet. Windshield shattered. Steering column displaced. Driver's door could not be opened.

**DRIVER / DECEASED:**
- Helen Ruth Marsh, female, age 27, of Boston
- Massachusetts operator's license: M-831-662

**ACCIDENT DESCRIPTION:**

At approximately 3:40 PM on October 14, 1958, the above-described vehicle was traveling northbound on Route 1 approximately 2.3 miles south of Wiscasset village. Road conditions were wet from intermittent rain throughout the day. The road at this location curves gently to the right and descends a moderate grade.

The vehicle appears to have failed to negotiate the curve. Tire marks indicate the vehicle crossed the centerline, then overcorrected to the right, leaving the paved surface. The vehicle crossed approximately 15 feet of gravel shoulder and struck a large oak tree (estimated 24 inches diameter) on the passenger side front quarter. The force of impact rotated the vehicle approximately 90 degrees counterclockwise.

The driver and sole occupant was found unresponsive in the driver's seat by Mr. Thomas Darcy of Bath, a passing motorist who stopped at approximately 3:45 PM. Mr. Darcy ran to the nearest residence (Connors farm, 1/4 mile north) and telephoned for assistance. Dr. Allan F. Webster of Wiscasset arrived at approximately 4:00 PM and pronounced Miss Marsh dead at the scene.

**CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:**
- Wet road surface
- Possible excessive speed (estimated 45-50 mph in 35 mph zone, based on skid marks and vehicle damage)
- No evidence of mechanical failure
- No evidence of alcohol involvement
- No other vehicles involved
- Driver was traveling alone

**PERSONAL EFFECTS RECOVERED:**
- Purse containing wallet ($14.32 in cash), Massachusetts driver's license, lipstick, compact, handkerchief
- Overnight bag in rear seat containing toiletries and one change of clothing
- Bouquet of flowers (chrysanthemums, now wilted) on passenger seat — possibly for grave visit

**NEXT OF KIN:** Unable to locate. No emergency contact in wallet. Employer (Pemberton & Associates, Boston) notified. They reported no known family. Funeral arrangements made through Sullivan Funeral Home per employer's direction.

**CASE STATUS:** Closed — Accidental death.

*[Signed] Patrolman David C. Reynolds*

[PRODUCTION NOTE: The bouquet of chrysanthemums on the passenger seat is a quietly devastating detail — Helen was bringing flowers to her parents' graves in Kittery. She was alone in life and alone in death, and then her identity was stolen by a woman who needed her name more than Helen had needed it herself.]

---

**DISCREPANCIES IDENTIFIED BY CATHERINE MARSH:**

1. **Maiden name confusion:** Real Helen's maiden name WAS Marsh. "Helen" told Mrs. Callahan her maiden name was "Stewart." William introduced her as "Helen Hennessey." Neither is correct.

2. **Hometown error:** Real Helen grew up in Kittery, not Kennebunk. "Helen" claimed Kennebunk — possibly because she had never been to Kittery and thought a different Maine town would be less verifiable.

3. **Parents' burial:** "Helen" claimed parents buried at Green Lawn Cemetery, Kennebunk. Real Helen's parents: Pine Grove Cemetery, Kittery. Catherine checked — no Marshes at Green Lawn.

4. **Religion:** Real Helen was Protestant. "Helen" (Vera) was raised Catholic by the Sisters of Mercy but unfamiliar with current practice — didn't know the Mass responses, held the rosary incorrectly.

5. **Wedding flowers:** "Helen" said lilies; William said roses. A bride who forgets her wedding flowers may be performing a role rather than living a memory.

6. **The gloves:** Helen always wore leather gloves, even indoors. Real Helen — no mention of this habit in any record. Vera may have worn gloves habitually to avoid leaving fingerprints, or simply as a psychological barrier — another layer of performance.

**CONCLUSION:** The evidence is overwhelming. William married a ghost.

## Evidence Piece 8: Death Certificate Witness Note

[PRINTED ON: Cold Case Club analysis paper.]

**COLD CASE CLUB — INVESTIGATOR'S NOTE**
**Cross-Reference: Death Certificate Witness**

The death certificate of Helen Ruth Marsh (Evidence Piece 1) lists a witness: **Frank J. Callahan**, identified as an employee of Sullivan Funeral Home.

Callahan is a common surname in the Boston area. However, investigators familiar with other Cold Case Club files may note that **Detective Francis "Frank" Callahan** appears in Case #1 as the lead investigator of the Ashford, Oregon disappearance. While there is no confirmed connection between these individuals, the coincidence of the surname — appearing on the document that proves the identity theft at the heart of Case #3 — is noted for the record.

Some coincidences are just coincidences. Some are threads that connect stories we haven't finished reading yet.
