**KEY DISCREPANCIES IDENTIFIED BY CATHERINE MARSH:**

1. **Maiden name:** Real Helen's maiden name WAS Marsh (coincidence — no relation to Crestfall Marshes). "Helen" told Mrs. Callahan her maiden name was "Stewart." William introduced her as "Helen Hennessey." Neither name is connected to the real Helen.

2. **Hometown:** Real Helen grew up in Kittery, not Kennebunk. "Helen" claimed Kennebunk — possibly chosen because she knew less about Kittery and thought a different Maine town would be harder to check. Catherine checked: no Helen Hennessey in the Kennebunk High School yearbook.

3. **Parents' burial:** "Helen" claimed parents buried at Green Lawn Cemetery, Kennebunk. Real Helen's parents were buried at Pine Grove Cemetery, Kittery. Catherine checked Green Lawn — no Marshes there.

4. **Religion:** Real Helen was Protestant. "Helen" (Vera) was raised Catholic but unfamiliar with Catholic practice (noted by Mrs. Callahan at St. Brendan's). This seems contradictory until you consider that Vera left St. Agnes at 18 and likely stopped practicing, while trying to maintain the fiction of being Catholic to match what she'd told William.

5. **Wedding flowers:** "Helen" forgot her own wedding flowers (lilies vs. roses). A small detail, but Catherine noticed it. A bride who forgets her flowers may be a woman performing a role rather than living a memory.
