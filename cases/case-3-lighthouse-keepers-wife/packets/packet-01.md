## Evidence Piece 1: Case Reopening Letter

[PRINTED ON: Cold Case Club letterhead. Heavy cream stock. Typed in serif font with red "CLASSIFIED" stamp across top corner.]

**COLD CASE CLUB**
*Case File #003*

Dear Investigator,

The file you are about to open has been closed for over sixty years.

On the morning of March 9, 1962, a lighthouse keeper named Edward Marsh discovered the body of his wife, Catherine, at the base of the cliffs below Dunmore Point Lighthouse in Crestfall, Maine. She was twenty-nine years old. She had been dead for approximately eight hours.

The local constable — a decent man with limited resources and no experience with violent death — ruled it an accident. Catherine, he concluded, had gone walking on the cliff path in dense fog and lost her footing. The case was closed within two weeks.

Catherine's mother, Agnes Doyle, spent the remaining twenty-two years of her life insisting that her daughter did not simply fall. She wrote to the state police. She wrote to the attorney general. She wrote to two separate governors of Maine. No one listened. Agnes died in 1984 with a box of her daughter's belongings under her bed and the word "murder" on her lips.

We believe Agnes was right.

Over the next six months, you will receive twelve evidence packets containing the original investigative materials, witness statements, medical records, personal correspondence, and documents that Constable Hale either overlooked or never obtained. Some of these materials have never been examined together. Some contradictions have never been noticed.

Your task is to determine what truly happened on the night of March 8, 1962, on the cliff path at Dunmore Point.

Who killed Catherine Marsh? And why?

The evidence will guide you. Trust your instincts. Question everything — especially the official story.

Good luck, Investigator. Catherine has been waiting a long time.

*Cold Case Club — Investigative Division*

## Evidence Piece 2: Original Incident Report

[PRINTED ON: Aged goldenrod paper. Typewriter font (Courier). Official form layout with fields. Some words x'd out and retyped, as if done on a manual typewriter. Coffee ring stain in upper right corner. Stamp: "CASE CLOSED — MARCH 22, 1962"]

**INCIDENT REPORT**

**Case No.:** 62-0041
**Date of Report:** March 9, 1962
**Reporting Officer:** Constable Robert J. Hale, Badge #3
**Incident Type:** Unattended Death — Accidental

**DECEASED:** Catherine Anne Marsh (née Doyle)
**Date of Birth:** June 14, 1933
**Age:** 29 years
**Address:** Dunmore Point Lighthouse, Lighthouse Road, Crestfall, Maine
**Occupation:** Homemaker
**Next of Kin:** Edward T. Marsh (husband), Agnes M. Doyle (mother)

**DATE OF INCIDENT:** March 8, 1962 (estimated)
**DATE DISCOVERED:** March 9, 1962, approx. 0615 hrs
**LOCATION:** Base of cliff, north face, Dunmore Point, Crestfall, Maine

---

**NARRATIVE:**

At 0632 hours on March 9, 1962, I received a telephone call from Edward T. Marsh, lighthouse keeper at Dunmore Point, reporting that he had discovered the body of his wife, Catherine A. Marsh, at the base of the cliff approximately 40 yards north of the boathouse stairs.

I arrived at the scene at 0745 hours. Mr. Marsh was present at the cliff base with the body. He stated he had climbed down to his wife upon discovering her and had been with her for approximately one hour before calling for assistance. The body had been moved from its original position — Mr. Marsh stated he had turned her over and held her.

**DESCRIPTION OF SCENE:** The deceased was found on the rocks at the base of Dunmore Point cliff, approximately 80 feet below the cliff path. She was wearing a wool coat (dark green), a gray dress, stockings, and one shoe (brown leather, low heel). The second shoe was recovered on the cliff path above, approximately 12 feet from the cliff edge, at a point where the path narrows and the ground is uneven. No railing or barrier exists at this section of the path.

**WEATHER CONDITIONS (night of March 8):** Dense fog reported throughout the day and evening. Visibility significantly reduced. [SEE CONSTABLE'S NOTE: Weather conditions were assessed based on general reports from town. Lighthouse weather log was not consulted.]

**PERSONS PRESENT AT LIGHTHOUSE COMPOUND, NIGHT OF MARCH 8:**
1. Edward T. Marsh — husband, lighthouse keeper. States he was in the tower on evening watch from approx. 1930-0600 hrs.
2. William R. Marsh — brother-in-law, visiting from Portland. States he retired to the guest room at approx. 1930 hrs and consumed whiskey. States he slept through the night and was unaware of any disturbance.
3. Helen V. Marsh — sister-in-law (wife of William), visiting from Portland. States she went to bed at approx. 2100 hrs. States she heard nothing unusual.

**PHYSICAL EVIDENCE:** The deceased sustained injuries consistent with a fall from significant height, including multiple fractures and contusions. Dr. Thomas Greer examined the body at scene and at his office (see attached medical report, to follow). Dr. Greer noted bruising on the deceased's upper arms but stated this could be consistent with impact during the fall.

**CONCLUSION:** Based on the physical evidence, witness statements, and weather conditions, it is my determination that Catherine A. Marsh died as a result of an accidental fall from the cliff path at Dunmore Point while walking in conditions of severely reduced visibility due to fog.

No evidence of foul play was found at the scene. No further investigation is warranted at this time.

**Case Status:** CLOSED — Accidental Death
**Date Closed:** March 22, 1962

*Signed,*
*Constable Robert J. Hale*
*Crestfall Police Department*
