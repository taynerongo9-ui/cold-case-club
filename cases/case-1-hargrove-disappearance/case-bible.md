# Case #1: The Hargrove Disappearance — Story Bible

## Classification
- **Case Number:** CC-001
- **Case Name:** The Hargrove Disappearance
- **Status:** Cold Case — Reopened 2024
- **Jurisdiction:** Ashford, Oregon
- **Original Date:** January 14, 1987
- **Original Ruling:** Presumed Suicide (February 1987)
- **Reopened:** March 2024 (Anonymous Tip)

## THE FULL STORY

### What the World Knows

Eleanor Hargrove, 34, wife of prominent Ashford real estate developer Richard Hargrove, was last seen at 11:42 PM on January 14, 1987, exiting the Monarch Theatre after the opening night performance of *A Doll's House*. Her navy peacoat was found draped over the railing of Morrison Creek Bridge the following morning. Her 1984 Volvo 240 remained in the theatre parking lot, keys in the ignition. No body was ever recovered.

Police conducted a 19-day search of Morrison Creek and the surrounding area. The investigation was led by Detective Frank Callahan of the Ashford Police Department. Richard Hargrove was briefly named a "person of interest" but was never formally charged. The case was ruled a presumed suicide on February 2, 1987, citing Eleanor's "history of emotional distress" — a characterization her family vehemently disputed.

The Daily Herald ran its final story on November 3, 1987: "LOCAL WOMAN MISSING — Police have suspended the search for Eleanor Hargrove, 34... Husband remains 'person of interest.'"

### What Actually Happened

In late 2086, Eleanor Hargrove discovered that her husband Richard had been systematically embezzling from their joint real estate portfolio. Over 18 months, Richard had funneled approximately $340,000 through a shell company called "Cascade Holdings LLC" — a company registered to a P.O. box in Portland with Victor Hargrove listed as sole director.

Eleanor found the discrepancy while reviewing financial documents for her art gallery's insurance renewal. She confided in her best friend and gallery business partner, Lillian Chen, in early January 1987. Lillian urged Eleanor to consult a lawyer. Eleanor drafted a letter to attorney David Wexler but never sent it — it was later found in her safe deposit box at First Pacific Bank.

Eleanor also kept a coded diary — she'd been fascinated by cryptography since college. Her final entry, written in a Caesar cipher (shift of 7), decoded to: "RICHARD KNOWS I FOUND THE ACCOUNTS. HE LOOKED AT ME DIFFERENTLY AT DINNER. I NEED TO MOVE THE EVIDENCE BEFORE HE DOES SOMETHING."

On the evening of January 14, Eleanor attended the opening night of *A Doll's House* at the Monarch Theatre — a play about a woman who discovers her husband's financial deception and ultimately leaves him. (The irony was not lost on Eleanor; Lillian later said Eleanor had chosen to attend specifically because of the parallel.)

Eleanor checked her **camel winter coat** at the theatre's coat check at 7:48 PM and received stub #47. She was seen by multiple witnesses throughout the evening.

After the performance, Eleanor was seen at The Stagecoach tavern at approximately 10:15 PM. Bartender Margaret Ellis served her a gin, no ice. Margaret later told police: "She was alone. Ordered gin, no ice. I remember because she kept looking at the door like she was waiting for someone who never showed up."

Eleanor was waiting for Daniel Cross, the theatre director, with whom she'd been having an affair for approximately four months. Cross never arrived — he was at the after-party at the theatre and didn't receive Eleanor's message until the following day.

Eleanor left The Stagecoach at approximately 11:30 PM and was captured on a single security camera exiting the Monarch Theatre's side entrance at 11:42 PM — she had returned to retrieve her camel coat from coat check.

What happened next was never captured on camera.

Richard Hargrove had discovered that Eleanor knew about the embezzlement. Earlier that day, Eleanor had confronted him at their home, telling him she was "going to make it right." Richard called his brother Victor, and they agreed Eleanor had to be stopped before she went to authorities.

Richard intercepted Eleanor in the theatre parking lot. He convinced her to come to their lake house property (the Hargrove Lake Property, 40 minutes outside Ashford on Route 26) to "talk things through." Victor was already waiting there.

At the lake house, the conversation escalated into a violent confrontation. Eleanor was killed between 12:45 AM and 1:15 AM on January 15. Thomas Brewer, the Hargrove estate groundskeeper who lived in a cottage on the property, heard loud arguing at approximately 1:15 AM — but Richard told police the next day that he and Eleanor had "argued on the phone" that evening, and Brewer, intimidated by his employer, didn't push back.

Richard and Victor buried Eleanor's body in a pre-dug foundation trench on a parcel of land Richard owned on the east side of Ashford. Six days later, on January 20, Richard filed construction permits for "Ashford Commons," a mixed-use development. The foundation was poured over Eleanor's remains within weeks.

To stage the suicide, Richard took Eleanor's everyday navy peacoat — which had been hanging in their front closet — and draped it over the Morrison Creek Bridge railing sometime between 2:00 and 4:00 AM. He drove Eleanor's Volvo from the lake house back to the Monarch Theatre parking lot, placed the keys in the ignition, and walked the 1.3 miles home.

The critical mistake: Eleanor had worn her **camel coat** to the theatre that night, not the navy one. The coat check stub (#47, issued at 7:48 PM) recorded "camel wool coat." The navy coat found on the bridge was a plant. But Detective Callahan never subpoenaed the theatre's coat check records, and the theatre disposed of unclaimed items — including the stub log — after 90 days. However, the coat check attendant, a college student named Patricia Yoon, kept a personal notebook of items checked that night because she was training and wanted to practice the system. That notebook survived.

### The 2024 Reopening

In 2024, Ashford Commons was slated for demolition to make way for a new development. During preliminary excavation, a construction worker noticed unusual soil composition in the original foundation layer. An anonymous tipster — believed to be an aging Victor Hargrove, now suffering from terminal cancer and guilt — sent a letter and photograph to the Ashford Police Department.

The case was reopened. Modern forensic analysis of the soil disturbance, combined with ground-penetrating radar, confirmed the presence of human remains. DNA testing confirmed the remains as Eleanor Hargrove.

Richard Hargrove, now 79, was arrested on March 15, 2024. Victor Hargrove died of pancreatic cancer on April 2, 2024, three weeks after the arrest. A confession letter from Victor was found among his personal effects by his daughter.

## CHARACTER PROFILES

### Eleanor Hargrove (Victim)
- **Age:** 34
- **Occupation:** Owner/operator of Hargrove Gallery (fine art, downtown Ashford)
- **Background:** Born Eleanor Addams in Portland. BA in Art History from Reed College. Moved to Ashford in 1978 after marrying Richard. Opened gallery in 1982 with Lillian Chen.
- **Personality:** Intelligent, cultured, warm but increasingly isolated. Passionate about art and community. Privately unhappy in marriage for several years.
- **Key details:** Fascinated by cryptography (kept coded diary). Wore a distinctive emerald ring her mother gave her. Loved Ibsen — chose to attend *A Doll's House* deliberately.

### Richard Hargrove (Killer)
- **Age:** 42 at time of murder; 79 at arrest
- **Occupation:** Real estate developer, Hargrove Development Corp.
- **Background:** Born in Ashford. Third-generation developer. Ashford Rotary Club president, youth baseball coach, churchgoer. The "golden son" of the Hargrove family.
- **Personality:** Charismatic, controlling, image-obsessed. Publicly devoted husband. Privately cold, calculating, prone to rages behind closed doors.
- **Motive:** Eleanor's discovery of the $340K embezzlement threatened his reputation, his freedom, and his upcoming Ashford Commons development.
- **Key details:** Left-handed. Drove a 1986 Ford Bronco. Had a key to the Monarch Theatre (donated to the renovation fund). Known to work late — alibi for night of Jan 14 was "working at the office until midnight."

### Victor Hargrove (Accomplice)
- **Age:** 39 at time of murder; died at 76
- **Occupation:** Business partner in Hargrove Development Corp., handled finances
- **Background:** Always in Richard's shadow. Less charming, more anxious. Did whatever Richard asked.
- **Personality:** Nervous, deferential to Richard, increasingly guilt-ridden over the decades.
- **Key details:** Listed as sole director of Cascade Holdings LLC (the shell company). Phone records show 3 calls between Richard and Victor on Jan 14 (4:12 PM, 8:30 PM, 11:55 PM). Terminal cancer diagnosis in 2023 prompted anonymous tip.

### Detective Frank Callahan (Original Investigator)
- **Age:** 45 in 1987; 82 in 2024
- **Occupation:** Lead detective, Ashford PD (retired 1998)
- **Background:** 22-year veteran. Good cop who was pressured by department brass and the Hargrove family's influence. Filed his final report with private reservations he never made public.
- **Personality:** Methodical, haunted by this case. Kept personal case notes separate from official file. Volunteered his notes when case reopened.
- **Key details:** His personal notes contain observations that contradicted the suicide ruling — including his suspicion about the coat and the too-convenient timing of Richard's alibi.

### Daniel Cross (Red Herring — The Lover)
- **Age:** 38
- **Occupation:** Artistic director, Monarch Theatre
- **Background:** Moved to Ashford from San Francisco in September 1986. Divorced. Genuinely talented director.
- **Personality:** Passionate, artistic, emotionally open. Genuinely loved Eleanor. Devastated by her disappearance.
- **Key details:** Had no alibi for after midnight (was alone at home after the after-party). Wrote love letters to Eleanor that police found. Richard publicly confronted him at The Stagecoach on Jan 10 — witnessed by Margaret Ellis and others. Cross is completely innocent but looks guilty.

### Margaret Ellis (Key Witness)
- **Age:** 29
- **Occupation:** Bartender, The Stagecoach tavern
- **Background:** Lifelong Ashford resident. Sharp, observant, no-nonsense.
- **Personality:** Direct, excellent memory, doesn't embellish.
- **Key quote:** "She was alone. Ordered gin, no ice. I remember because she kept looking at the door like she was waiting for someone who never showed up."
- **Key details:** Also witnessed the Richard/Daniel confrontation on Jan 10. Saw Eleanor leave at ~11:30 PM. Noted Eleanor was wearing a "nice tan coat, not her usual blue one."

### Lillian Chen (Eleanor's Best Friend)
- **Age:** 36
- **Occupation:** Co-owner, Hargrove Gallery
- **Background:** Met Eleanor at Reed College. Moved to Ashford to open the gallery together. Closest confidante.
- **Personality:** Loyal, fierce, analytical. Never believed the suicide story.
- **Key details:** Eleanor told her about the embezzlement on Jan 2. Lillian urged her to see a lawyer. Has kept Eleanor's gallery ledgers showing the financial discrepancies. Told police in 1987 but was dismissed as "emotional."

### Thomas Brewer (Red Herring — The Groundskeeper)
- **Age:** 52
- **Occupation:** Groundskeeper/caretaker, Hargrove Estate and Lake Property
- **Background:** Served 4 years for assault (1971-1975). Hired by Richard in 1980. Lives in cottage on Hargrove Lake Property.
- **Personality:** Quiet, guarded, intimidated by Richard. Good man with a past he can't escape.
- **Key details:** Heard arguing at 1:15 AM on Jan 15 at the lake property. Told police, but his criminal record was used to discredit him. Richard told police the sounds were from a "phone argument." Brewer was too afraid of losing his home and job to push back.

### Dr. Susan Park (Medical Examiner)
- **Age:** 41
- **Occupation:** County Medical Examiner
- **Background:** Board-certified forensic pathologist. Only 2 years into the position in 1987.
- **Personality:** Meticulous, principled, frustrated by the suicide ruling.
- **Key details:** Filed a memo noting that without a body, a suicide ruling was "premature and not supported by physical evidence." Her memo was buried. She retired in 2010 and gave a statement when the case reopened.

### Ruth Addams (Eleanor's Mother)
- **Age:** 61 in 1987
- **Occupation:** Retired schoolteacher, Portland
- **Background:** Widowed in 1979. Eleanor was her only child.
- **Personality:** Dignified, persistent, heartbroken. Never stopped fighting.
- **Key details:** Filed formal complaints about the investigation. Wrote letters to the governor. Told anyone who would listen that Eleanor would never have committed suicide. Died in 2019 without knowing the truth.

### Officer James Whitfield (Responding Officer)
- **Age:** 26 in 1987; 63 in 2024
- **Occupation:** First responding officer; now Chief of Police, Ashford PD
- **Background:** Fresh out of the academy in 1987. Now a 37-year veteran.
- **Personality:** Competent, carries guilt about the case. Made Chief in 2012.
- **Key details:** First on scene at Morrison Creek Bridge. His original report noted the coat was "neatly placed, not thrown" — suggesting staging. This observation was not emphasized in the final case file. Authorized the 2024 reopening immediately upon receiving the anonymous tip.

## RED HERRINGS — DETAILED

### 1. Daniel Cross's Affair
- **Introduced:** Packet 5
- **Why it's suspicious:** Gives Richard a "crime of passion" defense angle; Cross has no alibi after midnight; love letters prove emotional involvement
- **Why it's a red herring:** Cross genuinely loved Eleanor. He was at the after-party until midnight, then went home alone. He had no motive — Eleanor was planning to leave Richard for him.
- **Resolution:** Packet 12 confirms Cross was never a suspect in the reopened investigation.

### 2. Anonymous Threatening Letters
- **Introduced:** Packet 5
- **Why it's suspicious:** Suggests Eleanor had enemies, possibly a stalker
- **Why it's a red herring:** The letters were from Martin Hale, owner of a competing gallery in nearby Crestview. He was angry about losing an artist to Eleanor's gallery. Petty, not homicidal.
- **Resolution:** Mentioned briefly in Packet 12's final summary.

### 3. Thomas Brewer's Criminal Past
- **Introduced:** Packet 8
- **Why it's suspicious:** An ex-con living on the Hargrove property who "heard" something the night of the murder
- **Why it's a red herring:** Brewer's assault conviction was from a bar fight 16 years earlier. He'd been clean since. His testimony about the argument at 1:15 AM is actually one of the most important clues — it proves Eleanor was alive long after she supposedly jumped from the bridge.
- **Resolution:** Packet 12 vindicates Brewer completely.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

### Tone & Voice
- Detective notes: Terse, professional, matter-of-fact. Callahan writes like a cop — short sentences, observations without editorializing.
- Witness statements: Each character has a distinct voice. Margaret Ellis is direct and sharp. Thomas Brewer is hesitant, uses simple language. Richard Hargrove is smooth and controlled. Lillian Chen is emotional but precise.
- Newspaper clippings: Period-appropriate journalism style — formal, slightly sensational, small-town paper trying to sound bigger than it is.
- Eleanor's writing: Intelligent, literary, anxious. Her diary entries and letters reflect someone who reads widely and thinks carefully.
- Modern documents (2024): Clean, clinical, bureaucratic — forensic reports, DNA analysis, formal correspondence.

### Physical Production Guidance
- All 1987 documents should look aged — cream/off-white paper, typewriter font where appropriate
- Newspaper clippings: Newsprint-weight paper, columnar layout, Daily Herald masthead
- Handwritten items: Eleanor's diary, love letters, anonymous tip letter — use handwriting fonts or actual hand-lettering
- Official documents: Police letterhead, court stamps, bank watermarks
- The coded diary (Packet 9): Include a "cipher reference card" that looks like something Eleanor would have owned — perhaps a library bookmark or university reference card
- Modern 2024 documents: Clean white paper, laser-printed, modern letterhead

### Easter Eggs & Details
- The play *A Doll's House* mirrors Eleanor's situation perfectly — Nora discovers her husband's financial secrets
- Eleanor's gallery was showing an exhibition called "Hidden in Plain Sight" at the time of her disappearance
- The coat check stub number (47) is the same as Eleanor's age would have been in 2000
- Richard's shell company "Cascade Holdings" is named after the Cascade Range — hiding in plain sight in Oregon
- The construction permit for Ashford Commons lists the site address as 1987 Millbrook Road — the year Eleanor disappeared
