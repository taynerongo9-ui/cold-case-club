**[LETTER — shaky handwriting on plain white paper:]**

To the Chief of Police,

I don't know how to write this letter. I've been trying to write it for thirty-seven years and I never could. I'm writing it now because I'm dying and I don't want to take this to my grave.

Eleanor Hargrove did not kill herself.

I know this because I was there. Not when it happened — I wasn't in the room. But I was part of it. I helped. God forgive me, I helped.

She's not in the creek. She was never in the creek. She's at 1987 Millbrook Road. Under the foundation. The east corner, near where the loading dock was. That's where we — that's where she was put.

I know the building is coming down. I've heard they're demolishing it for the new development. When they take up the foundation, they'll find her. I'm telling you now so you know what to look for. So you can be there when it happens.

I enclosed a photograph. I took it last month when I drove through Ashford one last time. You can see the soil — even after all these years, the ground settled differently over that section. A foundation man would notice. I noticed.

I can't give you my name. Not because I'm trying to protect myself — it's too late for that. I'm dying. The doctor says weeks, not months. But there's someone else involved, someone still alive, and he has a family now. Grandchildren. What I'm about to do will destroy them. I accept that. But I can't put my name on it. Not yet. Maybe when I'm gone, someone will find my own letter and finish what I've started.

I'm sorry about Eleanor. She didn't deserve what happened. She was a good woman who found out something she wasn't supposed to find out, and she was punished for it. That's all it was. Money. It was always about the money.

I go to sleep every night and I see her face. I have for thirty-seven years. Maybe after this letter, I'll stop seeing it. Probably not.

Do the right thing. I couldn't. But you still can.

## Evidence Item 3: Modern Forensic Analysis Report

[PRODUCTION NOTES: Professional forensic report format — modern, clean, clinical. Oregon State Crime Lab letterhead. Technical language but readable. This is the scientific confirmation of what the anonymous tip alleged.]

---

**OREGON STATE POLICE**
Forensic Services Division — Crime Laboratory
13309 SE 84th Avenue, Clackamas, OR 97015

---

**FORENSIC ANALYSIS REPORT**

**Case Number:** APD 24-0301 (ref: 87-0114)
**Agency:** Ashford Police Department
**Requesting Officer:** Detective Sarah Chen
**Report Date:** March 12, 2024
**Analyst:** Dr. James Okoro, Forensic Anthropologist
**Reviewed by:** Dr. Patricia Albright, Laboratory Director

---

**BACKGROUND:**

On February 20, 2024, Ashford PD requested forensic analysis of a specific section of the concrete foundation at 1987 Millbrook Road, Ashford, OR (formerly "Ashford Commons"), based on an anonymous tip suggesting human remains might be present beneath the structure.

**METHODS EMPLOYED:**

1. **Ground-Penetrating Radar (GPR):** A 400 MHz antenna GPR survey was conducted over the east section of the foundation on February 20-21, 2024. The survey covered a 20x30 foot area centered on the location identified in the anonymous photograph.

2. **Soil Boring & Analysis:** Three test borings were drilled through the concrete foundation on February 25, 2024, at locations indicated by GPR anomalies. Soil samples were collected at depths of 2, 4, and 6 feet below the foundation surface.

3. **Controlled Excavation:** Based on GPR and soil boring results, a controlled excavation was conducted on February 28 - March 1, 2024, removing a 10x6 foot section of the concrete foundation and excavating to a depth of 5 feet.

4. **Skeletal Analysis & DNA:** Recovered remains were transported to the Oregon State Crime Lab for anthropological analysis, identification, and cause-of-death determination.

---

**FINDINGS:**

**GPR Results:**
The ground-penetrating radar survey identified a significant subsurface anomaly at a depth of approximately 3.5 feet below the foundation surface, in the east corner of the building approximately 8 feet from the former loading dock. The anomaly measured approximately 5.5 feet in length and 2 feet in width, consistent with a burial. The radar signature showed clear differentiation between the anomaly and surrounding soil, indicating disturbance of the original soil horizon.

**Soil Analysis:**
Soil boring #2 (at the center of the GPR anomaly) returned samples with elevated levels of:
- Adipocere (grave wax) — consistent with decomposition of human tissue in anaerobic conditions
- Calcium phosphate — consistent with bone mineral degradation
- Cadaverine and putrescine (trace) — decomposition byproducts

These chemical signatures are consistent with long-term interment of human remains.

**Excavation Results:**
A largely complete human skeleton was recovered at a depth of 3.5 feet below the concrete foundation surface. The remains were in an advanced state of decomposition consistent with approximately 37 years of interment in moist, clay-heavy soil sealed beneath concrete. Preservation was moderate — the concrete cover had created a relatively stable environment.

**Skeletal Analysis:**
- Sex: Female
- Age at death: 30-40 years (estimated from pubic symphysis and dental wear)
- Stature: Approximately 5'5" to 5'7"
- Ancestry: European
- Dentition: Full adult dentition present. Dental records comparison pending.
- Trauma: The hyoid bone is fractured. This finding is consistent with manual strangulation, though other causes cannot be definitively excluded given the state of decomposition.

**Artifacts recovered with remains:**
- One (1) emerald ring, gold setting, right hand (consistent with description in missing persons report #87-0114)
- Fabric remnants consistent with women's evening attire
- One (1) pair women's dress shoes, size 7.5

---

**IDENTIFICATION:**

**DNA Analysis:**
DNA was successfully extracted from the left femur. The DNA profile was compared to a buccal swab provided by Lillian Chen (as a reference to exclude) and to a stored blood sample from Ruth Addams (Eleanor Hargrove's mother, deceased 2019 — sample stored at Legacy Emanuel Medical Center from a 2017 blood draw).

**Result: Positive identification.**

The mitochondrial DNA of the recovered remains is a match to Ruth Addams's sample, confirming a maternal relationship. Nuclear DNA analysis is consistent with the remains being those of Eleanor Ruth Hargrove (née Addams), DOB 03/22/1952.

**Confidence level: 99.97%**

---

**CAUSE OF DEATH:**

Based on the hyoid fracture and the circumstances of interment (clandestine burial beneath a concrete foundation), the manner of death is classified as **HOMICIDE**.

Probable cause of death: **Asphyxia due to manual strangulation**, though the degree of decomposition prevents absolute certainty regarding mechanism.

---

**CONCLUSION:**

The remains recovered from 1987 Millbrook Road, Ashford, Oregon, are positively identified as Eleanor Ruth Hargrove, reported missing January 15, 1987. The manner of death is homicide. The remains were deliberately buried beneath the foundation of the Ashford Commons building, which was constructed in January-February 1987 by Hargrove Development Corporation.

[Signed]
Dr. James Okoro, Ph.D.
Forensic Anthropologist, Oregon State Crime Lab

[Signed]
Dr. Patricia Albright, Ph.D.
Laboratory Director

## Evidence Item 4: Victor Hargrove's Obituary

[PRODUCTION NOTES: Newspaper obituary format from The Oregonian, Portland's major newspaper. Standard obituary — no hint of the dark truth underneath. The disconnect between the bland public tribute and what we now know creates a powerful effect.]

---

**THE OREGONIAN — Portland, Oregon**
**April 5, 2024**

---

**OBITUARY**

**Victor Anthony Hargrove**
March 29, 1947 — April 2, 2024

Victor Anthony Hargrove, 76, of Portland, Oregon, passed away on Tuesday, April 2, 2024, at Providence Portland Medical Center following a battle with pancreatic cancer.

Born in Ashford, Oregon, to Arthur and Catherine Hargrove, Victor was a lifelong Oregonian with deep roots in the community. He attended Ashford High School, where he played basketball and was a member of the debate team. He earned a degree in business administration from the University of Oregon in 1970.

Victor spent his career in real estate development, working alongside his brother Richard as Vice President of Hargrove Development Corporation. Together, they built residential and commercial properties throughout Douglas County, contributing to the growth of Ashford and surrounding communities. Victor was known for his quiet diligence and attention to detail.

In his personal life, Victor was an avid fisherman and a devoted reader. He enjoyed spending time at his cabin in the Cascades and was a longtime member of the Portland chapter of Trout Unlimited.

Victor was preceded in death by his parents, Arthur and Catherine Hargrove, and his sister-in-law, Eleanor Hargrove. He is survived by his brother, Richard Hargrove of Ashford; his daughter, Sarah Hargrove-Mitchell of Lake Oswego; and two grandchildren, Emma (12) and Jack (9).

A private family service was held on April 6, 2024.

In lieu of flowers, the family requests donations to the Pancreatic Cancer Action Network.

---

**DET. SARAH CHEN — NOTE (attached, not for public release):**

*Victor Hargrove died seventeen days after we positively identified Eleanor's remains and twelve days before the grand jury convened.*

*He never testified. He never faced charges. He died in a hospital bed surrounded by his daughter and grandchildren, none of whom knew what he had done.*

*But he left something behind. His daughter, Sarah Hargrove-Mitchell, contacted us on April 8 — six days after his death. She had found a sealed envelope in his nightstand, addressed to the Douglas County District Attorney. She hadn't opened it. She brought it to us.*

*Inside was a seven-page handwritten confession. It will be entered as evidence in the prosecution of Richard Hargrove. The contents of that confession will be shared in the final packet.*

*Victor Hargrove carried his guilt for thirty-seven years. He wrote the anonymous tip. He wrote the confession. He died knowing both had been delivered.*

*I don't know if that constitutes redemption. That's above my pay grade.*

*— SC*

## Evidence Item 5: Detective Callahan's Statement — 2024

[PRODUCTION NOTES: A short typed statement from the now-82-year-old retired detective. Clean modern paper, typed (he doesn't handwrite anymore — arthritis). Brief but powerful.]

---

**Statement of Frank Callahan**
**Retired Detective, Ashford Police Department**
**March 16, 2024**

I got the call from Chief Whitfield yesterday evening. He told me they found her. He told me where.

I sat down in my kitchen and I cried for the first time in forty years.

I kept my case notes — every one of them — in a cardboard box in my garage since 1987. I always knew they'd matter someday. I just didn't know if I'd live long enough to see it.

The coat. The money. The timeline. The groundskeeper's testimony. The brother's alibi. The construction permits. I had all of it. I had the whole picture. What I didn't have was a body, and what I didn't have was a department willing to look where the evidence pointed.

I want to say something about Ruth Addams. Eleanor's mother. She fought for her daughter every day until she died in 2019. She wrote letters. She made phone calls. She stood outside the police station in the rain. She never stopped.

Ruth died without knowing the truth. That's the thing I can't forgive. Not myself, not the department, not this town. Ruth Addams deserved to know, and we failed her. We all failed her.

But Eleanor has been found. And the man who killed her is going to answer for it. That has to be enough. It has to be, because it's all we have.

I don't have anything else to say. My notes speak for themselves. I hope you can hear them now, because I've been shouting into the wind for thirty-seven years.

[Signed] Frank Callahan
Ashford, Oregon
