## Evidence Item 7.2 — Witness Statement: Thomas Pruitt, Waiter

[PRINT FORMAT: Typed witness statement on Charleston PD form. Handwritten signature at bottom. This is the critical alibi-breaking document. The tone should be that of a young, nervous man trying to be helpful and accurate.]

---

CHARLESTON POLICE DEPARTMENT
WITNESS STATEMENT

**Case:** 93-HOM-0842
**Date:** December 14, 1993
**Witness:** Thomas James Pruitt
**DOB:** 04/08/1971
**Occupation:** Waiter, Charleston Yacht Club (part-time); Student, College of Charleston
**Address:** 88 Coming Street, Apt. 3, Charleston, SC 29403

**Statement taken by:** Det. Lorraine Voss, Badge #2847

*I, Thomas James Pruitt, provide the following statement voluntarily:*

---

I've been working at the Yacht Club as a part-time waiter since August 1992. I work mostly the big events — galas, regattas, member dinners. I was assigned to serve Tables 3 and 4 at the Autumn Gala on October 17th.

Table 4 was the Mayor's table. Mr. Tate was there with his wife. I know Mr. Tate because he's a regular at the club and he's always polite to the staff — tips well, remembers your name, that kind of thing.

Dinner service for Table 4 started around 8:15. Everything was normal through the main course. Senator Pinckney gave his speech around 9:00. After that, dessert and coffee were served. I was clearing dessert plates from Table 4 at about 9:30 or 9:35.

At that point, Mr. Tate was at the table. His wife was talking to Mrs. Middleton. Mr. Tate seemed distracted — he kept checking his watch. I noticed because he's usually very social, very engaged. That night he seemed like he was waiting for something.

I went to the kitchen to drop off the plates and pick up a coffee refill. When I came back to the floor — maybe five minutes later, so around 9:40 or 9:45 — Mr. Tate's chair was empty. His napkin was on the seat. Mrs. Tate was still there. I didn't think much of it — people step out for the restroom, to smoke on the veranda, whatever.

But he was gone for a while.

I kept serving the floor. I passed Table 4 several times. Each time I noticed, his chair was still empty. Mrs. Tate didn't seem worried — she was talking to the other ladies. But I remember thinking it was a long time to be gone from the Mayor's table.

The dancing started around 9:45 or 10:00. I was refilling water glasses and clearing the bar area. I didn't see Mr. Tate on the dance floor or at the bar or on the veranda.

Then, sometime around 10:25 or 10:30, I saw him come through the kitchen service corridor. I was picking up a tray of champagne flutes from the kitchen pass-through, and he came through the swinging door from the back. He was adjusting his bow tie and he looked — I don't want to say flustered, because Mr. Tate doesn't really get flustered. But he was walking fast. He didn't see me, or if he did, he didn't acknowledge me.

He went straight to the bar, got a scotch, and went back to Table 4. A few minutes later, the Post and Courier photographer came around and took a picture of him with the Mayor. I remember because Mr. Tate suddenly had his big smile back on. Like a switch.

So he was gone from the table from about 9:40-9:45 until about 10:25-10:30. That's roughly 45 minutes. Maybe a little less, maybe a little more.

I didn't think anything of it at the time. I heard about the hotel fire the next day but didn't connect it to Mr. Tate. Why would I?

Detective Voss came to the Yacht Club in December asking questions. She asked specifically about Mr. Tate's movements that night. I told her what I just told you. She asked if I was sure about the times, and I said I was as sure as I can be — I don't carry a watch, but I know the event schedule and I can place things relative to the speeches and the dancing.

I have nothing against Mr. Tate. He's always been good to me. I'm just telling you what I saw.

---

*Signed: Thomas J. Pruitt*
*Date: December 14, 1993*

*Witnessed: Det. L. Voss, Badge #2847*

---

[Voss's annotation on a separate note:]
*"Pruitt is credible. No motive to lie. He's a college kid working a side job — he doesn't move in Tate's circles and has no stake in this. His timeline matches the fire timeline perfectly. Tate left the gala at 9:45. Fire alarm at 10:12. Tate back at 10:30. That's enough time to drive to the Belmont, set the fire, and drive back. — LV"*
