### BACKGROUND

This analysis was requested as part of a re-examination of the circumstances surrounding the death of Marcus Webb in the Belmont Hotel fire of October 17, 1993. The original autopsy, conducted by Dr. Franklin Hayes, determined the cause of death to be smoke inhalation. A blood alcohol concentration of 0.14 g/dL was reported. **No comprehensive toxicological screening for drugs or sedatives was performed.**

At the request of Detective Voss (retired) and with authorization from the Charleston County Coroner's Office, preserved tissue and fluid samples from the original autopsy — maintained in the Coroner's evidence archive per standard retention protocols — were released to this laboratory for re-analysis.

### METHODOLOGY

Samples were subjected to comprehensive toxicological screening using:
- Immunoassay screening (EMIT/FPIA)
- Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS)
- Liquid chromatography-tandem mass spectrometry (LC-MS/MS)
- Headspace gas chromatography (for volatiles)

Analyses targeted over 300 compounds including common drugs, medications, sedatives, hypnotics, and their metabolites.

### RESULTS

**CONFIRMED FINDING: CHLORAL HYDRATE AND METABOLITE DETECTED**

| Analyte | Sample | Concentration | Reference Range |
|---------|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Trichloroethanol (TCE) | Blood (W-1) | 38 mcg/mL | Therapeutic: 5-15 mcg/mL |
| Trichloroethanol (TCE) | Liver (W-3) | 44 mcg/mL | — |
| Trichloroacetic acid (TCA) | Blood (W-1) | 12 mcg/mL | — |
| Trichloroacetic acid (TCA) | Urine (W-4) | 8 mcg/mL* | — |

*Concentration may be lower than actual due to sample degradation over time.

**BLOOD ALCOHOL:** Confirmed at 0.12 g/dL (original report: 0.14 — slight variance within expected degradation margin for 31-year-old samples).

**ALL OTHER SCREENS:** Negative for opiates, benzodiazepines, barbiturates, amphetamines, cocaine metabolites, and common over-the-counter medications.

### INTERPRETATION

**Chloral hydrate** is a sedative-hypnotic drug historically used as a sleeping agent. It is rapidly metabolized to trichloroethanol (TCE), its active metabolite, which produces sedation and unconsciousness. TCE is further metabolized to trichloroacetic acid (TCA).

The blood TCE concentration of 38 mcg/mL is **approximately 2.5 to 3 times the upper therapeutic range.** At this concentration, the subject would have experienced:

- Profound sedation progressing to unconsciousness
- Depressed respiratory drive
- Muscular relaxation and inability to ambulate
- Suppressed pain response and arousal reflexes

**At this dosage, combined with a blood alcohol level of 0.12 g/dL, Marcus Webb would have been completely unconscious and physically incapable of self-rescue at the time of the fire.**

The simultaneous presence of alcohol and chloral hydrate at these concentrations produces a synergistic sedative effect historically known as a "**Mickey Finn**" or "knockout drops." This combination has been documented in criminal poisonings since the late 19th century.

### SIGNIFICANCE

The presence of chloral hydrate in the victim's system at supra-therapeutic concentrations, combined with alcohol, establishes that Marcus Webb was chemically incapacitated at the time of the fire. This finding is:

1. **Inconsistent with accidental death.** A man who has been drugged into unconsciousness cannot accidentally start a fire with a cigarette — particularly a man who does not smoke.

2. **Consistent with deliberate incapacitation.** The dosage suggests intentional administration by a third party with the goal of rendering the victim unconscious.

3. **A critical omission from the original investigation.** The failure of the original autopsy to screen for sedatives is a significant deficiency. Had this analysis been performed in 1993, the case would almost certainly have been classified as homicide.

### CONCLUSION

Marcus Allen Webb was drugged with chloral hydrate prior to his death. The drug was most likely administered orally, dissolved in an alcoholic beverage (consistent with witness statements that Webb consumed bourbon on the evening of October 17, 1993). The combination of chloral hydrate and alcohol rendered Webb unconscious and unable to escape the fire.

**This finding, in my professional opinion, changes the manner of death from accidental to homicide.**

**RECOMMENDATION: This case should remain open and be reclassified as a suspicious death pending further investigation.**

**SUMMARY OF FINDINGS:**

After two weeks of investigation into the death of Marcus Allen Webb at the Belmont Hotel on October 17, 1993, I have identified the following facts that are inconsistent with the official accidental death ruling:

**1. DUAL FIRE ORIGIN.** Chief Warren Briggs's fire investigation identified two separate and independent origin points in Room 416. Accidental fires have a single point of origin. Two origin points indicate arson. (See Briggs report, CFD Incident #93-4417.)

**2. ACCELERANT.** SLED laboratory analysis confirmed the presence of petroleum-based accelerant at both fire origin points. No legitimate source for this accelerant has been identified in the room's contents or the hotel's inventory.

**3. THE CIGARETTE.** The victim was a lifelong non-smoker, confirmed by his physician, his wife, hotel staff, and personal friends. No cigarettes, lighter, matches, or ashtray were found in the room. The single cigarette found near the victim's hand was almost certainly placed there.

**4. SECURITY SYSTEM.** The fourth-floor security monitoring system was deliberately disabled at 9:38 PM via remote dial-in access using an owner-level authorization code. This occurred 34 minutes before the fire alarm was activated. Only two people had owner-level codes: the victim and his business partner, Gerald Tate.

**5. INSURANCE.** Gerald Tate increased the hotel's property insurance from $800,000 to $2,300,000 in September 1993 — one month before the fire. The partnership agreement requires both partners' written consent for insurance changes exceeding $100,000. No written consent from Webb has been produced.

**6. FINANCIAL IRREGULARITIES.** Preliminary review of hotel financial records suggests discrepancies in vendor payments that warrant further investigation. Attorney Samuel Cross has indicated that Webb was preparing financial documentation for submission to the IRS.

**7. TATE'S ALIBI.** While Gerald Tate attended the Charleston Yacht Club gala on the night of the fire, I have identified a potential gap in his timeline that requires further witness interviews. [NOTE: Pruitt's statement was obtained in December, after this report was filed.]

**RECOMMENDATION:**

The physical evidence, the security system record, the insurance increase, and the financial irregularities collectively point toward a deliberate act. This case should not be closed.

I request authorization to:
- Subpoena complete hotel financial records
- Obtain phone records for the Belmont Hotel and Gerald Tate's personal line
- Interview additional Yacht Club gala attendees regarding Tate's movements
- Request a comprehensive toxicology screen on the victim's preserved samples
- Consult with SLED arson investigators

**— Det. Lorraine Voss, Badge #2847**
