## Evidence Item 2.1 -- Reconstructed Timeline of October 17, 1993

[PRINT FORMAT: Typed document on Charleston PD letterhead. Courier font. Stapled pages. Header reads "SUPPLEMENTARY DOCUMENT -- Det. L. Voss, Badge #2847." Compiled from multiple witness statements and official records.]

---

CHARLESTON POLICE DEPARTMENT
CRIMES AGAINST PERSONS UNIT
SUPPLEMENTARY DOCUMENT

**Case:** 93-HOM-0842 (initially classified 93-ACC-1106)
**Subject:** Timeline reconstruction, October 17, 1993
**Prepared by:** Det. Lorraine Voss, Badge #2847
**Date:** October 28, 1993

The following timeline has been compiled from witness interviews, hotel records, fire department logs, and physical evidence. Times are approximate unless confirmed by mechanical or electronic records.

**AFTERNOON**

3:15 PM -- Gerald Tate arrives at the Belmont Hotel. Signs the front desk visitor log. States purpose: "reviewing monthly accounts." (Per front desk clerk Nancy Whitfield.)

3:20 PM - 4:45 PM -- Tate is in the fourth-floor business office (Room 420). He is also observed on the fourth floor near Room 416 by housekeeper Eva Chen at approximately 3:40 PM. When asked, Tate stated he was "checking on the room for Marcus." [NOTE: Room 416 was Marcus Webb's personal office and retreat. He kept it permanently reserved.]

4:50 PM -- Tate departs the Belmont. Signs out at front desk.

**EVENING**

5:45 PM -- A call is placed from Room 416 to the hotel's room service kitchen, ordering dinner: grilled flounder, rice pilaf, sweet tea. The call is logged by the kitchen at 5:47 PM. [NOTE: The dinner order was placed before Webb arrived at the hotel. However, this is consistent with Webb's known habit of calling ahead from his car phone -- one of the few mobile devices available in 1993 -- to have dinner waiting when he arrived. His car phone records would later confirm a call placed to the Belmont's main number at 5:44 PM.]

6:00 PM -- Marcus Webb arrives at the Belmont Hotel. Enters through the main lobby. Brief conversation with front desk clerk Nancy Whitfield. Whitfield states Webb appeared "normal, maybe a little tired." Webb proceeds to Room 416.

6:15 PM -- Room service delivers a dinner tray to Room 416. (Per room service log: grilled flounder, rice pilaf, sweet tea, complimentary cornbread. Delivered by room service attendant James Cooper.) Cooper states Webb answered the door in his shirtsleeves, smiled, and said "Thank you, James."

6:30 PM -- Gerald Tate returns to the Belmont briefly. Signs in at front desk. States he is "dropping something off for Marcus." Goes to the fourth floor. Duration of visit: approximately 15 minutes. [NOTE: Tate later states he shared a bourbon with Webb from Webb's personal decanter in Room 416. This is the probable point of sedative administration.]

6:45 PM -- Tate departs the Belmont. Signs out. Nancy Whitfield notes he appeared "cheerful, maybe even more so than usual. He whistled on his way out." [NOTE: Whitfield's observation was not included in the initial case summary.]

7:25 PM -- Tate arrives at the Charleston Yacht Club for the Autumn Gala. (Per Yacht Club valet log. Vehicle: black Mercedes 560SL, logged in at 7:18 PM.)

8:00 PM -- Raymond Hicks begins his shift as night manager at the Belmont. Conducts standard walk-through of all floors. Notes "all quiet" on floors 3, 4, and 5 in the shift log. Webb's light visible under the door of Room 416. Hicks does not knock.

8:30 PM -- Delilah Monroe begins her set in the Blue Room Lounge, first floor. Standards night -- Gershwin, Porter, Ellington.

9:00 PM -- Senator Pinckney delivers keynote at the Yacht Club gala.

9:15 PM -- Guest registers at front desk for Room 418 under the name "David Crane." Pays in cash. No luggage bell service requested. (Later identified as journalist Martin Hale.)

**THE CRITICAL WINDOW**

9:38 PM -- The Belmont Hotel fourth-floor security monitoring system goes offline. The system log records the shutdown as an authorized remote access via the hotel's dial-in phone interface. Access code used: Owner-level authorization. [NOTE: Only two people had owner-level codes -- Marcus Webb and Gerald Tate.]

9:45 PM -- (Estimated) Gerald Tate is last seen at the Yacht Club gala before an approximately 45-minute absence. (Per waiter Thomas Pruitt -- see Packet 7.)

10:00 PM -- (Estimated) Fire is set in Room 416. [Exact time undetermined.]

10:12 PM -- Fire alarm activated on the fourth floor. Manual pull station in east corridor, activated by guest Robert Delacroix (Room 412), who observed smoke in the hallway.

10:14 PM -- Raymond Hicks places 911 call from the front desk.

10:17 PM -- Charleston Fire Department dispatched. Engine Company 7, Engine Company 12, Ladder Company 3, Battalion 2.

10:22 PM -- Engine Company 7 arrives on scene.

10:30 PM -- (Estimated) Tate returns to the Yacht Club gala. (Per Pruitt statement.)

10:42 PM -- Gerald Tate photographed with Mayor Dalton at the gala. (Charleston Post and Courier society photographer.)

11:15 PM -- Fourth-floor fire contained. Room 416 destroyed. Rooms 414 and 418 sustain smoke and water damage.

11:45 PM -- Body recovered from Room 416. Male, severe burn damage. Preliminary identification based on room assignment and physical characteristics.

**OVERNIGHT / NEXT DAY**

12:30 AM (Oct 18) -- Patricia Webb notified by Charleston PD at her home on Tradd Street.

7:00 AM -- Positive identification: Marcus Allen Webb, W/M, DOB 03/15/1935. ID confirmed through dental records.

8:15 AM -- Gerald Tate contacted by Charleston PD. Expresses shock. His voice on the phone is described by the notifying officer as "steady."

9:00 AM -- Fire Chief Warren Briggs begins investigation of Room 416.

---

[Handwritten note at bottom of page, Voss's handwriting:]
*"Tate was at the hotel at 3:40 -- near 416 -- and again at 6:30 for 'bourbon.' He whistled on his way out. Then the security system goes down with an owner code at 9:38 and Tate disappears from the gala at 9:45. I don't believe in coincidences. This is a man executing a plan. -- LV"*

## Evidence Item 2.5 -- First Responder Field Notes

[PRINT FORMAT: Handwritten on a small spiral-bound notepad page, reproduced. Messy but legible handwriting. Pencil. Some smudging. The kind of notes a firefighter scribbles on scene.]

---

ENGINE CO. 7 -- FIELD NOTES
Firefighter/Paramedic Daniel Rojas
October 17, 1993

Arrived Belmont Hotel 22:22. Heavy smoke from 4th floor E-side windows. Flames visible Room 416 area. Two windows -- orange glow, active fire.

Lobby full of smoke -- maybe 2 ft visibility at ceiling. Hotel staff had evac'd most guests to King St. sidewalk. Night manager (Hicks) met us at door. Said one person unaccounted -- room 416, hotel owner.

Went up stairwell with Lt. Frazier and Chen. Stairwell smoky but passable to 3rd floor. 4th floor landing -- zero visibility. Heat intense -- I could feel it through my turnout coat. This fire was RUNNING. Crawled east corridor. Conditions deteriorating fast.

Reached 416 door. Fully involved. Door compromised -- charring visible, handle hot through gloves. Forced entry.

Room fully engulfed. Ceiling partially collapsed near window wall. Flashover had occurred -- everything in that room was involved. Not just fabric and paper, everything. The heat had calcined the plaster on the walls -- you could see the calcium deposits. That means sustained temps above 1,000 degrees. A cigarette fire in a hotel room does not do that. Not without help.

Found victim on floor between settee and door. Approximately 8 ft from entry. Face down. Severe burns. Position suggests victim was lying on settee and either fell or rolled to floor. No signs of crawling or movement toward the door -- no drag marks, no indication of attempted self-rescue. This man was unconscious when the fire reached him.

Could not safely extract immediately. Backed out. Ladder 3 vented roof. Engine 12 on external attack.

Returned to 416 at approx 23:30 after knockdown. Recovered victim at 23:45.

**Personal observations:**

- Fire damage heaviest at TWO locations in room -- near window/curtain area AND near entry closet/hallway door. Unusual for single-source ignition. I have never seen an accidental fire with two clearly separated origin points in a single room. In my seven years on the job, a two-point origin means one thing.

- V-pattern burn marks clearly visible on the east wall (origin point A) and the west wall near the closet (origin point B). Both V-patterns originate at floor level or near-floor level, indicating ignition source at the base. This is inconsistent with a dropped cigarette on a settee, which would produce a top-down burn pattern.

- Smell: accelerant. Something chemical under the smoke. Petroleum-based. Not just wood and fabric. I've been to enough car fires and garage fires to know what accelerant smells like. This room had accelerant.

- Room 416 damage significantly worse than you'd expect from a cigarette. This burned HOT and it burned FAST. The flashover occurred within minutes -- probably 8-10 minutes from ignition. Normal room fires take 20-30 minutes to reach flashover without accelerant involvement.

- The sprinkler heads in 416 activated but couldn't suppress the fire. Accelerant-fed fires overwhelm standard wet-pipe sprinkler systems because the rate of heat release exceeds the system's cooling capacity.

- No cigarette pack, no lighter, no matches, no ashtray found in room during initial survey. Just one cigarette near the victim's hand.

Reported observations to Chief Briggs on scene.

---
