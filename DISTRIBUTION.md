## BUSINESS MODEL JUSTIFICATION

### Why These Prices Work

```
MONTHLY: $14.99/mo (6 months = $89.94 total)
├── COGS per packet: ~$2.50 (paper, printing, stationery, artifacts)
├── Postage per packet: ~$1.20 (USPS First Class)
├── Cost per month (2 packets): ~$7.40
├── Gross margin per month: $7.59 (50.6%)
└── PURPOSE: Anchor price. Makes prepaid look like a steal.

PREPAID: $74.99 one-time (effective $12.50/mo)
├── Total COGS over 6 months: ~$44.40
├── Gross profit: $30.59 (40.8%)
├── Cash day 1: $74.99 (funds next customer's ads)
├── Net after $50 CPA: $24.99 profit on day 1
├── Churn: 0% (already paid)
└── PURPOSE: The growth engine. Every sale funds the next acquisition.

GIFT: $74.99 one-time
├── Same economics as prepaid
├── Recipient can't cancel (structural anti-churn)
├── 1 gift buyer often triggers 2-3 more purchases
├── LTV of gifted recipients: ~$180 (they buy for themselves)
└── PURPOSE: Viral loop + highest LTV customers.
```

### Unit Economics Target

| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Blended AOV | $82 | Mix of monthly ($14.99) + prepaid ($74.99) |
| COGS per customer | $44.40 | 12 packets over 6 months |
| Gross margin | 46% | After COGS |
| CPA (Meta ads) | $40-50 | Industry standard for niche subscription |
| Breakeven | Day 1 (prepaid) or Month 3 (monthly) | |
| LTV | $180 | Repeat purchasers buy Case #2 |
| LTV:CAC | 3.6:1 | Healthy benchmark is 3:1 |
| Email % of revenue | 38-50% | Target from Flower Letters model |
| Yearly churn | <5% | Stories + gifts = structural retention |

### Why $74.99 and not $99 or $49?

- **$49 kills your margins.** After $44 COGS + $50 CPA, you're losing money.
- **$99 reduces gift volume.** The sweet spot for impulse gifting is $50-85. Above that, people comparison shop.
- **$74.99 is psychologically under $75.** It feels like a "deal" without needing a discount code.
- **The Flower Letters charges $100 prepaid for 12 months.** You're charging $75 for 6 months. Per-month, you're comparable. But a 6-month commitment feels lighter than 12 months for a first-time buyer.

## DISTRIBUTION CHANNELS — RANKED BY PRIORITY

### CHANNEL 1: Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram) — 60% of Budget

**Why first:** The Flower Letters built their entire $7M business on Meta ads. It's the proven acquisition channel for niche subscription products targeting women 35-55.

**Phase 1: Validation ($20/day for 2 weeks = $280)**
- Run 3 ad variants from `ads/meta-ad-copy.md`
- Target true crime + mystery interests
- Optimize for email signups (not purchases)
- Goal: 200+ emails at <$1.50 per email
- If you hit this, the market is validated. Scale.

**Phase 2: Conversion ($50/day for 4 weeks = $1,400)**
- Switch optimization to purchases
- Use winning ad from Phase 1
- Target lookalike of email subscribers
- Goal: 30+ prepaid sales at <$50 CPA
- If CPA is below $50, you're profitable day 1. Scale harder.

**Phase 3: Scale ($100-200/day)**
- Launch Mother's Day campaign (see ads/meta-ad-copy.md)
- Add retargeting layer for site visitors
- Build lookalike audiences from purchasers
- Test video ads (hands opening evidence packets)

**Key rules:**
- Always show price in ad copy (qualifies clicks)
- Always include the guarantee
- Creative featuring real product photos outperforms mockups 3:1
- Refresh creative every 2-3 weeks (ad fatigue)

### CHANNEL 2: Email Marketing — 38-50% of Revenue

**Why critical:** Michael Clark said email drives up to 50% of Flower Letters' revenue. You own the list. Meta can't take it away.

**Email Sequence (automated — set up in Mailchimp, ConvertKit, or Loops):**

```
Trigger: New email subscriber

Day 0:  Welcome + free evidence preview (PDF attachment)
        Subject: "Your first piece of evidence is inside."
        Include: 1 witness statement, 1 newspaper clipping, 1 coded clue
        CTA: "Ready to start the full case? →"

Day 2:  The story behind Cold Case Club
        Subject: "Why we started sending mysteries through the mail"
        Personal story. Why you care. Why this exists.
        CTA: Soft — link to homepage

Day 5:  Social proof + testimonials
        Subject: "She pinned a suspect board to her office wall"
        3 testimonials. Photos if you have them.
        CTA: "Start your case →"

Day 8:  Gift angle
        Subject: "The gift they'll talk about for 6 months"
        Position as Mother's Day / birthday gift
        CTA: "Gift a case →"

Day 12: Urgency + guarantee
        Subject: "Still thinking about it? Here's what you're risking"
        Reframe: the cost of NOT buying (missing out on the experience)
        Highlight 30-day money-back guarantee
        CTA: "Start investigating — 100% risk-free →"

Day 18: Last chance
        Subject: "This is the last email about Case #1"
        Final push. Won't email about this again.
        CTA: "Get the full case for $74.99 →"
```

**Ongoing emails (manual, monthly):**
- New case announcements
- Behind-the-scenes of creating evidence packets
- Subscriber spotlight / community stories
- Seasonal gift reminders (Mother's Day, Father's Day, Christmas, Valentine's)

### CHANNEL 3: Organic Social (Instagram + TikTok) — $0 Budget

**Why:** Hannah (from the Flower Letters podcast) grew organically on TikTok. True crime content is massively viral on both platforms.

**Content pillars (post 3-5x/week):**

1. **Evidence reveals** — Film yourself opening an evidence packet. Slow, ASMR-style. Show each item. Don't spoil the story. Let people wonder.

2. **Cold case history** — Short-form videos about real unsolved cases (public domain). End with: "We turned a case like this into a 6-month mail experience."

3. **Behind the scenes** — Printing, stuffing envelopes, designing artifacts. People love seeing how things are made.

4. **Subscriber reactions** — Repost customer stories, suspect boards, book club photos. User-generated content converts 4x better than brand content.

5. **Coded message challenges** — Post a cipher. "Can you crack this in 24 hours?" First person to solve it in comments gets a free month. Drives engagement through the roof.

**Platform strategy:**
- Instagram: Reels (15-30s), carousels (evidence close-ups), Stories (daily)
- TikTok: Same Reels content, repurpose. True crime TikTok is enormous.
- Pinterest: Evidence packet flat-lays, gift guides. Pinterest is a search engine for gift ideas. High-intent traffic.

### CHANNEL 4: Referral Program — Lowest CAC

**Mechanic:**
- Every subscriber gets a unique referral link
- Refer a friend → both get a free bonus evidence packet (a "bonus clue" not in the main case)
- 3 referrals → free case tin
- Include referral card in every physical evidence packet ("Share this with a fellow detective")

**Why this works for Cold Case Club specifically:**
- Book clubs: 1 person subscribes, tells 5-8 friends. Group activity = built-in referral engine.
- Gift recipients: They finish their case and buy one for someone else.
- The physical mail IS the marketing. Every packet is a touchpoint that reminds them to share.

### CHANNEL 5: Podcast Ads — Targeted Reach

**Target shows:** True crime podcasts (Crime Junkie, My Favorite Murder, Casefile, Serial)
- These audiences are EXACTLY your customer
- Host-read ads convert 2-3x better than programmatic
- Typical cost: $20-30 CPM (cost per thousand listeners)
- Start with smaller shows ($200-500 per episode) to test

**Sample host-read script (30s):**
"This episode is brought to you by Cold Case Club. Imagine getting real evidence packets in your mailbox — detective notes, witness statements, coded messages — and piecing together a cold case over 6 months. It's like this podcast, except YOU'RE the detective. Go to coldcaseclub.com and use code [PODCAST] for free shipping on your first case."

### CHANNEL 6: Retail & Partnerships — Scale Play (Month 6+)

**Gift shops / bookstores:**
- Approach local mystery bookstores with a retail gift card (QR code → redemption page)
- Model: Flower Letters is in Newport Mansion gift shops
- Margin: wholesale at $40-45, retail at $74.99

**Subscription box partnerships:**
- Partner with existing mystery/book subscription boxes as an "upgrade" insert
- Include a sample clue card + discount code in their box
- Cross-pollination of similar audiences

**Corporate gifting:**
- Companies buy gifts for employees/clients
- Bundle pricing: 10+ cases at $60 each
- Custom corporate case with branded elements
